# Color & Lighting Vocabulary -- Seedance 2.0 Reference

> Seedance 2.0 has NO explicit color grading parameters. All color and lighting must be
> described narratively inside the prompt. The model responds strongly to specific,
> concrete lighting and color terminology. This reference is the canonical dictionary.

## Lighting Patterns (Portrait & Scene)

| Pattern | Description | Mood / Use |
|---|---|---|
| Rembrandt lighting | Key light at 45 degrees creating triangle of light on shadow-side cheek | Classic dramatic portrait, painting-like dignity |
| Butterfly lighting | Key light directly above and in front, creating butterfly shadow under nose | Glamour, beauty, fashion photography |
| Rim light / edge light | Light from behind subject creating bright outline separating subject from background | Dramatic separation, heroic silhouette, tension |
| Split lighting | Light illuminating exactly half the face, other half in complete shadow | Duality, inner conflict, dramatic character reveal |
| Broad lighting | Illuminated side of face turned toward camera, wider lit area visible | Flattering for thin faces, open and approachable |
| Loop lighting | Key light slightly off-center creating small shadow loop from nose onto cheek | Standard portrait, natural and versatile |
| Three-point lighting | Key light + fill light + back light working together for balanced illumination | Standard cinematic coverage, controlled and professional |
| Practical lights | Visible in-scene light sources (lamps, candles, screens) motivating the lighting | Naturalistic, immersive, grounded realism |
| Motivated lighting | Cinematic lighting justified by a visible or implied source in the scene | Believable dramatic lighting that feels natural |
| Cross lighting | Two lights from opposite sides creating rim on both edges, dark center | Intense confrontation, action, dramatic tension |
| Under lighting | Light source below the face, casting upward shadows | Horror, villain reveal, supernatural menace |
| Silhouette | Subject completely dark against bright background, shape-only information | Mystery, anonymity, iconic graphic moment |
| Kicker light | Accent light from behind and to the side, adding edge highlight on one side | Adds dimension and separation, subtle drama |
| Hair light | Dedicated light illuminating hair from above-behind, adding halo definition | Glamour, beauty, angelic quality |

## Light Quality Types

| Quality | Description | Visual Result |
|---|---|---|
| Hard light | Small or distant source, sharp defined shadows, high contrast | Crisp shadow edges, dramatic, stark |
| Soft light | Large or close source, gradual shadow falloff, low contrast | Gentle transitions, flattering, naturalistic |
| Diffused light | Light scattered through translucent material, even and wrapping | Shadowless, even coverage, overcast feel |
| Specular light | Highly directional, creates bright reflections on shiny surfaces | Glinting highlights, metallic shine, wet-look |
| Dappled light | Broken up by foliage or patterns, creating random light/shadow patches | Organic, forest canopy, natural movement |
| Volumetric light | Visible light beams in atmosphere (dust, fog, haze) | God rays, shafts of light, atmospheric depth |
| Ambient light | Non-directional environmental fill, no clear source | Flat, even, no strong shadows |
| Bounced light | Reflected off surfaces (walls, floors, reflectors) as secondary fill | Warmer fill, subtle, naturalistic secondary light |
| Raking light | Light at extreme angle across textured surface, emphasizing texture | Reveals surface detail, architectural drama |
| Flickering light | Intermittent or pulsing source (fire, candle, faulty electric) | Unease, warmth, instability, movement |

| Snow | Falling white particles, accumulated white ground fill | Bright bounce fill from below, particles catch light | "snow falling through light, bright ground bounce fill" |
| Steam / vapor | Rising translucent white plumes from heat sources | Localized volumetric diffusion, softens nearby lights | "steam rising through light creating localized volumetric glow" |

## Post-Processing Terms

| Term | Visual Effect | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Vignette | Darkened image corners drawing eye to center | Almost always; adds cinematic framing |
| Film grain | Organic noise texture simulating photochemical film | Period pieces, realism, texture, warmth |
| Electronic noise | Digital artifact pattern, banding, compression blocks | Surveillance, found footage, tech aesthetic |
| Chromatic aberration | Color fringe separation at edges (red/cyan split) | Distress, dream, vintage lens, disorientation |
| Lens distortion | Barrel or pincushion warping at frame edges | Wide-angle emphasis, GoPro feel, unease |
| Motion blur | Streaking on moving objects from slower shutter | Speed, disorientation, action, time passage |
| Slight overexposure | Bright, washed-out highlights bleeding into midtones | Dream, memory, divine, heavenly, heat |
| Film scratches | Vertical lines and dust marks over image | Vintage, damaged archive, found footage |
| Halation | Bright light bleeding/glowing beyond its boundary into dark areas | Romantic, dreamy, vintage lens character |
| Lens flare | Bright streak or polygon artifacts from direct light hitting lens | Epic, cinematic, sci-fi, Spielberg homage |
| Anamorphic streak | Horizontal blue-white flare line from anamorphic lens | Cinematic widescreen, prestige, sci-fi |
| Barrel distortion | Outward curve at wide angle, center bulge | Fisheye, surveillance, claustrophobia |
| Light leak | Random warm color bleed from film gate light exposure | Vintage, carefree, summer, analog warmth |
| Bloom | Soft glow around bright areas spreading into surrounding image | Fantasy, beauty, romantic, ethereal |
| Letterboxing | Black bars top and bottom creating widescreen aspect ratio | Cinematic authority, scope, prestige |

## Usage Notes for Prompt Construction

1. **Layer your description**: Start with the light source, then describe how light behaves in the atmosphere, then declare the overall color tone.
2. **Be specific about direction**: "light from upper left" is stronger than just "dramatic light."
3. **Name the look when possible**: "Blade Runner 2049 monochromatic orange wash" is more effective than describing the color from scratch.
4. **Combine film stock + grade**: "Shot on CineStill 800T with teal and orange grade" gives the model two reinforcing signals.
5. **End with post-processing**: Closing with "film grain, vignette, slight chromatic aberration" adds final texture and mood.
6. **Match lighting to mood**: Use the Mood Mapping table to select consistent camera + light + color + speed combinations.
7. **Atmospheric effects amplify lighting**: Adding "through fog" or "with dust particles" to any light description dramatically increases visual depth.
