## 1. Count-Up Number Effect (Money Counter)

Numbers constantly send a signal to your brain. When they start growing, you can't look away.

### How To:
1. Create a **text layer** > delete default text
2. Type a number (e.g., `1137572`)
3. Choose whatever font you like
4. Go **5 frames forward** (press right arrow 5 times)
5. **Cut** right there
6. Delete the part after the cut
7. Take the text and add it right next to it
8. **Select both** > copy and paste on the timeline
9. Repeat: 2 > 4 > 8 > **16 columns of text**
10. Change the numbers in every single column, making **small gradual jumps**
11. Last number = the final number (e.g., 1,000,000)
12. Add a **sound effect**

> **Important:** Always make small jumps. Don't go from 137K to 300K — that looks awkward. Scale up gradually.

## 2. Progression / Loading Bar

Psychological trigger: loading bars tell your audience something is coming. They add rhythm, urgency, and make your video more dynamic.

### How To:
1. Create a **text layer** > remove text > space it out
2. Select **Background** > white color (the base)
3. **Duplicate** the layer > change color to **green** (the progress)
4. Select both > **Create Compound Clip** (or Option+G on Mac)
5. Inside compound: put green on **layer two**
6. On the green layer: **Mask** > **Split** > rotate it
7. Place it at the beginning of the clip
8. Click **keyframe** > go to the end > drag the mask all the way
9. Play with the **feathering** for a more dynamic look
10. To smooth: right-click > **Show Variable Speed Animation** > select keyframes > **Quad Out**

### Customize the Duration:
- Create a text layer of the desired duration (e.g., 10 seconds)
- Set keyframe at the start and another at the end of the text layer

### Final Shape:
- Select both clips > **Create Compound**
- Go to **Mask** > **Rectangle** (or whatever shape)
- Round the corners however you like

## 3. 3D Flip Effect

This effect literally flips the screen and resets your viewer's attention. Best used for transitions or dynamic highlights. Quick, bold, breaks the rhythm.

### How To:
1. Place your logo/image
2. Go to **Animations** > scroll down to the **Flip series**
3. Use **Flip 4** (recommended)
4. Make it bigger

### Bonus — Freeze at a Specific Angle:
1. Create a **Compound Clip** from the flip
2. Now the **Freeze** option appears
3. Freeze at the desired position/angle
4. Delete the layers before and after
5. You now have a fixed 3D-angled logo/image

## 4. Gradient Text

Gradient effects instantly boost the quality of your content. White fonts and simple colors are good, but there's always something missing — that's exactly the gradient effect.

### How To:
1. Create a **text layer** (e.g., "PREMIUM")
2. **Duplicate** the layer
3. Choose your font
4. Layer 1: set to **yellow**
5. Layer 2: set to **orange**
6. Create **separate Compound Clips** for each layer
7. On the top layer: **Video** > **Mask** > **Split**
8. Drag the **Feathering** up (e.g., value 35)
9. Adjust mask position

### Bonus — Add Glow:
1. Select both compounds > create **another compound**
2. Go to **Effects** > search **"Glow Too"**
3. Drag the effect onto the compound
4. Lower the intensity so it's not too flashy

## 5. Shine Effect

Adds motion and glow to any element. Smooth, stylish, instantly upgrades the way your video feels.

### How To (on an Icon):
1. **Duplicate** the icon
2. On the copy: **Adjustment** > **Curves** > bring the line all the way to the top corner
3. Go to **Video** > **Mask** > **Film Strip**
4. Rotate the mask however you want
5. Play with **feathering** for more shine
6. If too intense: **Basic** > **Blend** > lower the value
7. Go to **Mask Setting** (scroll down)
8. Set a **keyframe** at the start
9. Go to the end > move the mask > set another keyframe
10. To smooth: **Variable Speed Animation** > select keyframes > **Rebound Out** (recommended)
11. Add a **shine sound effect**

### On Gradient Text:
- Copy one of the gradient layers
- Paste where needed > **Undo Compound**
- Change color to **white** (or desired shine color)
- Create compound > **Mask** > **Film Strip**
- Make it smaller, position it, animate with keyframes

## 6. Instant Cinematic Effect

Turns any boring footage into high-quality content in just a few clicks. Absolute instant movie trailer. No plugins, no LUTs, just drag and drop.

### How To:
1. Go to **Effects**
2. Search **"Cinematic"**
3. Select the **Cinema** effect
4. Drag it on the timeline
5. Make it the same length as the clip

> That's it. Really that easy.

## 7. Motion Tracking — Lock Text to Moving Subject

Lock your text or graphic onto a moving subject. Storytelling through movement — CapCut makes it super easy.

### How To:
1. Create a **text layer** (e.g., "Video Editing")
2. Make it the same duration as the clip (turn on **snap**)
3. Choose your text style/effect
4. Go to **Tracking** > **Motion Tracking**
5. Fit the subject inside the tracking box
6. Press **Start** and let CapCut do the work
7. For better smoothness: turn off **Scale** and **Distance**

## 8. Floating / Pendulum Effect

Floating animations always look like they've been made in After Effects. In CapCut it takes just a few seconds.

### How To:
1. Place your icon/text/image
2. Go to **Effects** > search **"Play"**
3. Use **Play Pendulum**
4. Drag it on the element
5. Decrease **Twist** and **Sharpen** for a cleaner look

### Fix for Cut-Off Edges (on circular subjects):
1. Remove the Play Pendulum effect
2. Create a **Compound Clip**
3. **Now** apply Play Pendulum on the compound
4. Edges won't be cut off anymore

### Fix for Glow with Edges:
- Same solution: remove glow > create compound > reapply glow

## 9. Text Behind Subject

Definitely adds depth and that After Effects look without touching After Effects. Fast, clean, instantly levels up your visuals. One of the easiest pro tricks in CapCut.

### How To:
1. Create a **text layer** (e.g., "TEXT")
2. Choose font and size
3. Position wherever you want
4. **Duplicate the video clip**
5. On the duplicate: use **Auto Removal** (remove background)
6. On the text: create **Compound Clip** (Option+G / right-click)
7. Go to **Video** > **Basic** > set the text on **layer two**
8. The text now appears **behind** the subject

## 10. Smooth Cinematic Zoom In

Variable speed animation gives you that cinematic moment in just seconds. One small detail that makes your edit feel more high-end, even if the footage is basic.

### How To:
1. Click on **keyframe** in **Transform**
2. Go a couple frames forward (where you want the zoom to end)
3. Add another **keyframe**
4. Zoom in the clip (e.g., **145%**)
5. Right-click > **Show Variable Speed Animation**
6. On the second keyframe: click **Auto Curve**
7. On the first keyframe: click **Auto Curve**
8. Drag the **blue line** of the second keyframe towards the front

### Speed Curve Explained:
- **Blue line towards the front** = starts super fast, then slows down (most natural)
- You can play with both keyframes for different effects:
  - Slow > fast > slow = very cinematic feel
