## 1. Layouts - Vertical vs Default

When you open CapCut, top right corner you'll see the layout option:
- **Default** = best for **16:9** videos (YouTube) — gives you enough room to work
- **Vertical** = best for **9:16** videos (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts)

**The trick:** If you're editing in 9:16, go to the option in the top right corner and switch from "Default" to **"Vertical"**. Switching to vertical makes a big difference in your workspace.

---

## 2. Rulers - Safe Zones for 9:16

Staying in the safe zone is crucial. Use **3 main rulers**:

| Ruler | Position | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Eye Level** | Horizontal at eye height | Set up your horizontal ruler here |
| **Captions** | Below the chin | Not too low — don't distract viewers |
| **Motion Graphics** | Center of screen | Where people's attention naturally goes when scrolling |

> This keeps things focused and easy to follow.

## 3. Custom Templates - Speed Up Your Workflow

**NOT** CapCut's viral templates. Templates **you build yourself**.

### How to create your template:
1. Start a **new project**
2. Import all your: sound effects, transitions, effects, assets — everything you ever use
3. Organize everything on the timeline
4. **Next time you edit:** create a duplicate and start editing in your pre-built template

> You save a lot of time because everything is lined up nicely on the timeline.

## 4. Keyboard Shortcuts - Edit Faster

### Delete with X (instead of Backspace)
- Change your delete key from Backspace to **X**
- Press **A** to deselect cutting mode
- Press **X** to delete
- Everything with your left hand — no need to move your right hand from the mouse

### Split All with S
- Place your playhead where you want the cut
- Press **S** = cuts **all layers** at once

### Delete Left/Right with Q and W
| Key | Action |
|-----|--------|
| **Q** | Deletes everything to the **left** of the playhead |
| **W** | Deletes everything to the **right** of the playhead |

> Instead of: B (cut) + X (delete) + A (deselect) = just press **Q**. Saves you 3 clicks every time. When you're editing long videos, those seconds add up fast.

## 5. Creating Motion Graphics in CapCut

### Example: Search Bar

**Step 1 — The bar base:**
1. Create a **text layer**, space it out, and size it up
2. Pick your color
3. Add a **mask** > choose **rectangle**
4. Adjust it to look like a bar
5. Round the corners

**Step 2 — The search icon:**
1. Create another text layer and type "O"
2. Set the color
3. Create another text layer with the same color
4. Mask it with rectangle to create the effect

> You can really make everything in CapCut. Video editing is about creativity, and creativity is a skill. The more you practice, the better it gets.
